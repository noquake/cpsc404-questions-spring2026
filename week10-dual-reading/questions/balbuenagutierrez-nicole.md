Week 10 Dual-Reading - Version Control vs. Live Transaction Risk

Producing Open Source Software explains that open-source communities can rely on informal, consensus-based decision-making because version control allows technical mistakes to be easily undone. Because actions aren't permanent, developers have the freedom to test ideas without formal approval.

On the other hand, Help Your Boss Help You highlights that corporate managers operate under a constant fear of unfixable mistakes reaching upper management. In a corporate setting like an investment banking deal, sending a flawed financial model to a client is permanent and cannot simply be "rolled back.” 

Does the permanence and financial risk of corporate deliverables inherently require the strict, top-down chain of command described in Help Your Boss Help You? Or, could a high-stakes corporate team actually reduce their error rate by adopting the "open-source" model of consensus-based peer review?
