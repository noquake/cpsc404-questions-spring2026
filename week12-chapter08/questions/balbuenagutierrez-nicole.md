This chapter distinguishes between being "friendly" and being "friends," noting that while "friends are added selectively," the workplace requires a specific type of professional distance to avoid emotional hurt when business decisions, such as raises or promotions, become competitive. In high-pressure environments, how does an employee effectively signal "loyalty" and "having the back" of both their manager and their co-workers without crossing into a "special friendship" that risks oversharing or perceptions of favoritism?
