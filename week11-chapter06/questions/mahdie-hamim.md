# Week 11 – HYBHY Chapter 6: Communicating More Effectively. 

**Author:** Hamim Mahdie

## Question

From my internship experience, I’ve seen how much progress can depend on how you communicate with a manager when especially when they’re blocking something. Chapter 7 talks about adapting to your manager using ideas like the Four Scales (e.g., task vs. people focus) and even personality frameworks like the Keirsey Temperament Sorter, while Producing Open Source Software shows that influence comes more from visible contribution and community trust than formal authority.

So in practice, if you’re trying to push back on a bad decision, how does “adapting your communication style” change depending on the system—i.e., tailoring your approach to your boss in a corporate chain of command versus building credibility and persuading publicly in an open source project? And does open source really make it easier to work around ineffective leadership, or does it just shift the burden from managing up to constantly proving yourself to the community?
