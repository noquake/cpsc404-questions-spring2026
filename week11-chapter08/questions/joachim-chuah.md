### Question
“You said that trying to become friends with your boss exposes you to ‘significant traps and vulnerabilities,’ so why is maintaining emotional distance more valuable than building a closer personal relationship if trust and loyalty are supposed to be central to working well together?”

### Context
In many offices, a startup esepcially, people try to build personal relationships with their boss - grabbing coffee or sharing personal updates - because it can seem like it leads to more trust and opportunities. And in a startups case, because the team is so small, sharing these things could also be inevitable. Over time, though, those boundaries can blur, and what feels like a friendship to you is still a professional, power imbalanced relationship to them. When conflict arises, that mismatch can backfire, making situations feel more personal and risky than they actually are.
